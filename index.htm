The Importance of Deep Roots

But blessed is the one who trusts in the Lord,
whose confidence is in him.
They will be like a tree planted by the water
that sends out its roots by the stream.
It does not fear when heat comes;
its leaves are always green.
It has no worries in a year of drought
and never fails to bear fruit.

Jeremiah 17:7-8

Even if a tree is planted, there are trees that are uprooted and trees that remain. For example, if you a plant a tree in a desert, where it does not get much rain, it is not able to grow deep roots. Thus, when it finally does rain, at the slightest opposition from the wind and water, a tall tree is able to be toppled over. However, if we deeply plant our roots, we will not be shaken even when waves come crashing.
